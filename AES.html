Advanced Encryption Standard (AES)

Aim Objectives Procedure Theory Simulation

Aim

The primary aim of AES is to provide a secure method for encrypting data, ensuring confidentiality and integrity by using symmetric-key block cipher techniques.

Objectives

Data Confidentiality: Protect data from unauthorized access.
Data Integrity: Ensure that data is not modified during transmission or storage.
Efficiency: Provide fast and efficient encryption and decryption processes.
Flexibility: Support various key sizes (128, 192, and 256 bits) for different security needs.

Procedure

Key Expansion:

The encryption key is expanded into a set of round keys.

Data Preparation:

> The plaintext is divided into fixed-size blocks (typically 128 bits).

Initial Round:

AddRoundKey:

The plaintext block is XORed with the initial round key.

Main Rounds (1-9):

>

SubBytes:

Substitution of each byte with a value from an S-box.

ShiftRows:

Circular shift of rows by different offsets.

MixColumns:

Linear transformation of each column.

AddRoundKey:

XOR with the round key.

Final Round:

SubBytes:

Substitution of each byte.

ShiftRows:

Circular shift of rows.

AddRoundKey:

XOR with the final round key.

Output:

The final encrypted block.

Theory

Security

AES is widely considered secure due to its robust design and large key sizes. However, side-channel attacks and quantum computing pose potential future threats.
