Caesar Cipher

Aim Objectives Procedure Theory Simulation

Aim

The Caesar Cipher is one of the simplest and oldest methods of encrypting messages. This technique involves shifting the letters of the alphabet by a fixed number of places. For example, with a shift of three, the letter ‘A’ becomes ‘D’, ‘B’ becomes ‘E’, and so on.

Objectives

The objectives are :
Convert plaintext to cipher text The main goal of the Caesar cipher is to convert readable text (plaintext) into an unreadable form (cipher text).
Simple Encryption The Caesar Cipher is one of the earliest and simplest methods of encryption3. It was used by Julius Caesar to protect his communications during military operations.
Foundation for Modern Cryptography The Caesar cipher introduced the idea of substituting letters, which is a basic principle in cryptography and laid the foundation for more advanced encryption methods.
