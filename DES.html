DES (Data Encryption Standard)

Aim Objectives Procedure Theory Simulation

Aim

The aim of studying the Data Encryption Standard (DES) is to understand its design, implementation, and operational mechanisms as a symmetric-key block cipher, while evaluating its historical significance and security implications in modern cryptography.

Objectives

Understand the Structure: To explain the architecture of DES, including its block size, key size, and the Feistel network structure.
Analyze Encryption and Decryption Processes: To detail the steps involved in encrypting and decrypting data using DES.
Evaluate Security Features: To assess the strengths and weaknesses of DES, particularly its key length and susceptibility to various attacks.
Explore Historical Context: To investigate the development of DES and its impact on cryptographic practices and standards.
Compare with Modern Standards: To compare DES with newer encryption standards like AES (Advanced Encryption Standard) regarding security and efficiency.

Procedure

Key Generation:

Generate a 64-bit key for encryption, noting that only 56 bits are used for the actual encryption process.

Initial Permutation:

Apply an initial permutation to the plaintext before starting the encryption rounds.

16 Rounds of Processing:


For each of the 16 rounds, perform a series of substitutions and permutations using S-boxes and P-boxes.
Use a round key derived from the main key for each round through a key scheduling algorithm.

Final Permutation:

After completing all rounds, apply a final permutation to produce the cipher text.

Decryption Process:

The decryption process uses the same steps as encryption but applies the round keys in reverse order.
